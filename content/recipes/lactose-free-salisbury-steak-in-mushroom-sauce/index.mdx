<Ingredients>

### Ingredients

- **For the mince**
  - 500 g beef mince
  - ½ egg
  - ½ onion
  - 1 x clove garlic
  - ½ tsp white vinegar
  - ½ tsp salt
  - ½ tsp black pepper
  - ½ tsp dried thyme
  - ¼ tsp garlic powder
- **For the mushroom sauce**
  - 50 g mushrooms
  - ½ onion
  - 60 ml coconut cream
  - ½ cube beef stock
  - 1 tbsp oil

</Ingredients>

<Instructions>

### Instructions

#### Preparation

- Finely chop the fennel.
- Crush the garlic.

#### Method

- **Step 1**
  - In a large bowl, mix the beef mince, egg, garlic, fennel, vinegar, salt, black pepper, thyme and garlic powder.
  - Mix everything by thoroughly squishing it through your fingers.
  - When the texture of the meat becomes smooth, it's ready.
  - Pat into four equal size steaks.
- **Step 2**
  - Heat the lard in a large frying pan on medium heat. (Number 6).
- **Step 3**
  - When the lard is hot, carefully place the steaks into the frying pan.
  - Fry 10 mins on one side.
  - Carefully turn them over and brown them on the other side - a further 10 mins.
  - When the juices run clear, the steaks are done. Remove, set aside and cover.
- **Step 4**
  - Add the olive oil to the sauce using the same frying pan.
  - Fry the fennel and mushrooms until soft; stir often.
  - Add the coconut cream and the beef stock cube and stir well.
  - When the coconut cream is warm, and the stock cube has dissolved, remove the sauce from the heat.
- **Step 5**
  - Serve one salisbury steak per person with a quarter of the sauce.

</Instructions>
